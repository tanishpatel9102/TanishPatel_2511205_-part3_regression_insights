# Final Recommendation

## Overview

The objective of this analysis was to identify the business factors most strongly associated with monthly sales across retail stores. Both simple and multiple regression models were developed, and the multiple regression model was selected because it provides a better explanation of sales performance.

---

## Factors Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales

The regression analysis indicates that the following variables have the strongest relationship with monthly sales:

* **Footfall** – Stores with higher customer traffic generally achieve higher monthly sales.
* **Customer Rating** – Better customer satisfaction is associated with improved sales performance.
* **Marketing Spend** – The simple regression model shows that increased marketing investment is positively associated with monthly sales.
* **Average Discount Percentage** – The model suggests that discount strategy influences sales performance and should be managed carefully.

---

## Variables Leadership Should Focus On

Based on the regression results, leadership should prioritise:

* Increasing customer footfall through effective marketing campaigns.
* Improving customer experience to maintain higher customer ratings.
* Monitoring marketing investment to maximise return on investment.
* Using discount strategies selectively rather than relying on excessive price reductions.

---

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Regional dummy variables (South, East, and West) were not consistently statistically significant. Therefore, leadership should avoid making major strategic decisions based solely on store location. Additional operational and market factors should also be considered.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

* Increase marketing activities that attract more customers to stores.
* Improve customer service and store experience to maintain strong customer ratings.
* Ensure adequate inventory availability to reduce lost sales opportunities.
* Regularly monitor sales performance and update regression models as new data becomes available.

---

## Risks and Limitations

* The regression model explains a large portion of sales variation but does not capture every factor influencing sales.
* External factors such as seasonality, competitor actions, economic conditions, weather, and local events are not included in the model.
* Some predictor variables may change over time, requiring periodic model updates.

---

## Association Does Not Prove Causation

Regression analysis identifies statistical relationships between variables and monthly sales, but it does not prove that one variable directly causes another. For example, higher marketing spend may be associated with higher sales, but other factors such as seasonal demand or promotional campaigns could also contribute. Business decisions should therefore combine regression findings with operational knowledge and market experience.

---

## Final Recommendation

The **Multiple Linear Regression** model is recommended because it explains substantially more variation in monthly sales than the simple regression models. Leadership should use this model to support data-driven decisions while recognising that regression provides evidence of association rather than definitive proof of cause and effect.
